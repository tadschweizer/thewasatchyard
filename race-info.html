The Backyard Ultra Format

A backyard ultra is an ultramarathon race where competitors must complete a 4.167‑mile loop every hour, on the hour. The race continues until only one runner remains.

Each lap contains:

  • 4.167 miles of mixed terrain (one yard = 100 miles in 24 hours)
  • Technical trail sections with rocks and roots
  • Scenic mountain views throughout
  • Single‑track and wider trail combinations
  • Aid station access at start/finish

The Rules

  • Each loop must be completed in under 60 minutes
  • All runners start together at the beginning of each hour (the "whistle")
  • Your rest time is whatever remains of the hour after finishing the lap
  • Miss the start of an hour (DNF) or fail to complete a lap in time and you're eliminated
  • The race ends when only one runner completes a lap that no one else finishes
  • Lap‑specific prizes awarded throughout the day to celebrate milestones

Lap Timing & Strategy

The backyard ultra format is as much mental as it is physical. Here's what makes it unique:

  • Faster runners have more rest: complete the lap in 45 minutes, rest for 15 minutes
  • No set finish time: the race could last 12 hours or 48+ hours
  • Night running: bring headlamps and be prepared for darkness
  • Crew support: use your rest time wisely for nutrition, gear changes, and recovery

Important: The mountain terrain and unique format make this extremely challenging. Proper training, mental preparation, and a solid support crew are essential. Most runners will not finish beyond 12–24 hours.

Aid Station & Support

A fully stocked aid station will be available at the start/finish area between laps.

  • Water and electrolyte drinks
  • Energy gels, bars, and real food options
  • Medical support on site 24/7
  • Dedicated crew area for personal support teams
  • Covered rest area with seating
